Mesh Surface Emitter

Emits particles on the surface of a mesh. Note, that the auto_direction has a meaning in this emitter. If auto_direction is set to true, the direction is based on the generated normals. The 'angle' property provides a variation on this direction.
The Mesh Surface Emitter and the Vertex Emitter resemble, but there are differences. The Vertex Emitter follows the contours of a mesh along the edges. The Mesh Surface Emitter however, generates the emission points random on the surface. Depending on the distribution type, it uses the edges, the vertices, or the whole surface of the mesh. The latter means that particles are emitted on the triangles of which the mesh consists.

Definition

Syntax:

emitter   MeshSurface   [emitterName]   {emitter properties}

See ParticleEmitter for explanation of the syntax.

Example: 

emitter                         MeshSurface
{
    emission_rate               5000
    force_emission              true
    mesh_name                   ogrehead.mesh
    mesh_surface_distribution   heterogeneous_2
    time_to_live                10
}

Properties


Mesh Name

Defines the name of the mesh from which particles are emitted. Syntax:

mesh_name   #name

mesh_name is the keyword. mesh_surface_mesh_name is deprecated from version 1.2
#name is the name of the mesh.

Distribution

This emitter identifies several types of distribution. These distributions are:

  • edge - particles emit random on the edges
  • heterogeneous_1 - distribute particles over the surface of the mesh, but relatively more particles on the smaller faces
  • heterogeneous_2 - same as above, but now more particles are emitted on the larger faces
  • homogeneous - distribute particles homogeneous (random) on the mesh surface
  • vertex - particles only emit from the vertices

Syntax:

mesh_surface_distribution   edge | heterogeneous_1 | heterogeneous_2 | homogeneous | vertex

mesh_surface_distribution is the keyword.
edge | heterogeneous_1 | heterogeneous_2 | homogeneous | vertex defines on of the possible distribution type. Default: homogeneous.

Scale

It is possible to set the scale of the mesh. This scale is independent of the scale of the particle system itself. This means that both scales are taken into account. Syntax:

mesh_surface_scale   #x #y #z

mesh_surface_scale is the keyword.
#x represents the x-value of the scale.
#y represents the y-value of the scale.
#z represents the z-value of the scale.
