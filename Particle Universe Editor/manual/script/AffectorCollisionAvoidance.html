Collision Avoidance Affector

This affector is one element of the Flocking behaviour algorithm. It adjusts the direction of a particle if it comes too close within the range of its neighbouring particles. The method of detecting whether particles are nearby is not done by a brute-force method, but with a spatial hashing algorithm. This means that in the Particle Technique, you have to set the spatial hashing properties (i.e. 'spatial_hashing_cell_dimension').

Definition

Syntax:

affector   CollisionAvoidance   [affectorName]   {affector properties}

See ParticleAffector for explanation of the syntax.

Example: 

affector                        CollisionAvoidance
{
   avoidance_radius             100
}

Properties


Avoidance Radius

This is the maximum distance between 2 particles before the avoidance function comes into action. This means that if the positions of two particles < Avoidance Radius, the directions of both particles are adjusted. Note, that it is called radius, because it is comparable with a bounding sphere collision detection. Syntax:

avoidance_radius   #value

avoidance_radius is the keyword.
#value is the maximum distance between 2 particles, before the directions are adjusted. Default: 100.
