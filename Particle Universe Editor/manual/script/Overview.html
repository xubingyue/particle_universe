Overview

The Particle Universe System consists of a number of key components, the building blocks. This is a brief summary.

ParticleSystemManager

The ParticleSystemManager basicly has the same function as the one in Ogre's ParticleFX plugin. It manages creation and deletion of other components, it manages aliases, ParticleSystem templates and forms the entry point for compiling the particle scripts.

ParticleSystem

A ParticleSystem is the most top level of a particle structure, that consists of Particles, ParticleEmitters, ParticleAffectors, ParticleObservers, etc. The ParticleSystem can be seen as the container that includes the components that are needed to create, display, move and affect Particles.

Particle

The Particle is the element where its all about. Particles are generated (emitted) from specific areas and their motion and behaviour is controlled by the components of which a ParticleSystem consists. Several types of Particles are possible, where the VisualParticle is the most obvious one, but also ParticleEmitters, ParticleAffectors and even ParticleTechniques can act as a Particle. This gives them typical Particle characteristics. Consider a ParticleEmitter that is emitted itself. During its life as a Particle it behaves as a Particle, but it is also is a ParticleEmitter and therefor emits other Particles (either VisualParticles or other Particle types).

VisualParticle

A VisualParticle represents the Particle that is displayed on the screen. It is the only Particle type that is rendered. It also has attributes typical for an object that is rendered; examples are height, width and depth, but also colour is a typical 'visual' object attribute.

ParticleTechnique

In analogy of Ogre's material system, the ParticleTechnique is introduced. It adds an extra layer between the ParticleSystem and the ParticleEmitters, ParticleAffectors, etc. A ParticleTechnique has a number of benefits. With the use of a ParticleTechnique it is possible to combine Particles with different materials into one Particle System. Each ParticleTechnique has its own ParticleRenderer and uses its own material. By combining multiple ParticleTechniques into one ParticleSystem, it is possible to create complex ParticleSystems. Also Particle LOD (Level Of Detail) is possible with the use of the ParticleTechnique. Light-weight ParticleTechniques (using a Billboard renderer for example) can be activated if the ParticleSystem is further away, while more complex ParticleTechniques could be used if the camera is near.

ParticleRenderer

A ParticleRenderer is responsible for rendering visual particles. The particle itself usually doesn't define its appearance; the renderer determines how the particle will look like. This means that changing a renderer can imply that a totally different object is rendered. Several types of renderers are possible. A billboard- and a Box- renderer are two examples.

ParticleEmitter

ParticleEmitters are responsible for spawning (emitting) the particles.

ParticleAffector

Particle affectors modify (affect) particles in a Particle System over their lifetime.

ParticleObserver

ParticleObservers are used to observe whether a certain condition/event occurs. This condition is often related to the state of a Particle, but also certain situations regarding a ParticleTechnique, ParticleEmitter or even the ParticleSystem can be validated. If a certain event occurs (i.e. the particle collides), it can be detected by an observer that validates collision events.

ParticleEventHandler

A ParticleEventHandlers are used to perform a task in case a certain event happens. A ParticleEventHandler is associated with a ParticleObserver; The ParticleObserver signals what event occurs, while the ParticleEventHandler performs the action.

ParticleBehaviour

ParticleBehaviour makes it possible to add additional behaviour to a particle. While a ParticleAffector acts as an external 'force' on the particles, the ParticleBehaviour defines the internal behaviour of each particle individually. For example, 'wind' is typically implemented as a ParticleAffector, while 'physical characteristics' apply to the behaviour of the particle itself. It is possible to register multiple Behaviours to a Particle.

Extern

An Extern class forms a bridge between an external component and the ParticleUniverse plugin. An example of such an external component is a physics system - such as Ageia's PhysX plugin - with which it becomes possible to add physical behaviour to particles. Also Affectors that are attached to their own SceneNode can be identified as 'extern'. An external Affector makes it possible to affect a ParticleSystem based on the mutual distance between the SceneNode of the ParticleSystem and the SceneNode of the external Affector.
